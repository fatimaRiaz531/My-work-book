# Glossary

## Key Terms

### AI-Native Development

Software development practices fundamentally designed around AI capabilities and constraints, not merely enhanced by AI tools.

### Specification

A formal, machine-readable document that completely describes the requirements, behavior, and constraints of a software system.

### Spec-Driven Development (SDD)

A software development methodology where formal specifications precede implementation, enabling both human developers and AI systems to operate from a shared, precise understanding of requirements.

### Reproducibility

The capacity to independently verify research claims and replicate development processes using documented specifications and standardized tools.

### In-Text Citation

A reference to an academic source placed within the body of the text, identifying the author and year of publication according to APA 7th edition format.

### Prompt Engineering

The practice of designing and refining natural language inputs (prompts) to effectively communicate requirements to AI systems, particularly large language models.

### Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

A readability metric that estimates the U.S. grade level needed to understand a piece of text.
